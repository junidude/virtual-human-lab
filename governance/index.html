How VHL is organized

Governance

Virtual Human Lab is organized as an independent, non-commercial research association based in Korea, without corporate registration. Its bylaws describe voting Regular Members, a General Assembly, officers, records, and finance rules.

VHL is not part of any government agency, military organization, university, hospital, or company. Professional affiliations identify individuals only.

Organization

How the records describe VHL's organization.

These records show how decisions are made, who can represent the group, how members vote, how records and funds are kept, and why VHL continues even when membership changes.

Purpose

Defined academic mission

VHL works at the intersection of medicine, transcriptomics, bioinformatics, computational biology, and machine learning.

Rules

Bylaws

The bylaws define the name, purpose, members, General Assembly, officers, voting, records, finance, research standards, and dissolution.

Organs

General Assembly and officers

The bylaws place voting Regular Members in the highest decision-making body and set the roles of the Representative, Finance Officer, and Auditor.

Voting

Voting rules

Ordinary matters use quorum and majority rules. Bylaw changes, officer removal, expulsion, major agreements, and dissolution require higher approval thresholds.

Continuity

Membership changes do not end VHL

Admission, withdrawal, replacement, or loss of status of a member does not dissolve the association or transfer its records, accounts, domain, or assets.

Assets

Separate, non-distribution property

Funds and assets are used only for academic purposes. No surplus or residual asset is distributed to members, officers, advisors, or related persons.

Official professional profile

The Representative carries out General Assembly resolutions, manages official communications, and represents VHL within the authority granted by the bylaws.

  • Material funding, data access, intellectual-property, debt, and institutional agreements require collective approval.
  • No officer may present a member's employer, hospital, university, company, government agency, or military organization as a VHL sponsor without written authorization.
  • Advisors and project collaborators provide expertise but do not vote or represent VHL unless separately admitted as Regular Members.

Operating principles

Governance is recorded, not improvised.

Meetings

Minutes and records

General Assembly and material committee meetings are recorded internally with agenda, attendance, votes, conflict recusals, and resolutions.

Conflicts

Disclosure and recusal

Members with direct financial, employment, authorship, intellectual-property, disciplinary, or related conflicts disclose and do not vote on that matter.

Support

Documented terms

Money, cloud credits, GPU access, equipment, and data access are accepted only under documented terms that preserve scientific independence and compliance obligations.

Dissolution

No private residual distribution

After lawful liabilities and record duties are settled, remaining assets go to a non-profit academic, scientific, medical-education, or public-interest organization.

Governing documents

Public summary; private originals.

Public pages exclude signatures, private addresses, full minutes, account information, personal records, and protected research information.

What this page does not do

This website is a public summary. It does not replace signed founding records, member records, accounting records, or registrations that may be needed for a specific administrative purpose.

Public legal context

Korean court practice distinguishes a loose project from an unincorporated association by looking at the substance of the organization: purpose, rules, decision-making and representative bodies, majority-based operation, continuity despite member changes, and defined rules for representation, meetings, funds, and property management.

See also the Korean Civil Procedure Act Article 52, which recognizes litigation capacity for an unincorporated association or foundation with a representative or manager.
