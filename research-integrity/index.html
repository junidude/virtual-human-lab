Research practice

Research practice

VHL conducts independent academic research at the intersection of medicine, transcriptomics, bioinformatics, computational biology, and machine learning. Work should be reproducible, properly cited, and limited by the data-use terms, ethics review, institutional rules, and public-service obligations that apply.

Clinical boundary: VHL is not a medical provider and does not provide diagnosis, treatment, prescriptions, or patient-specific medical advice.

Operating principles

Working rules

Records

Reproducible methods

Work should keep enough record for others to understand the data, code, assumptions, citations, conflicts, and material corrections.

Authorship

Contribution, not title

Membership, seniority, funding, or advisory status alone does not create authorship. Authorship is based on actual scholarly contribution and responsibility for the work.

AI tools

Human accountability

Substantive use of generative AI or automated research systems is disclosed when required. Human authors remain responsible for accuracy, integrity, privacy, and compliance.

Independence

Affiliations are context

Professional affiliations identify people. They do not make an employer, hospital, university, company, agency, or military organization responsible for VHL work unless a written agreement says so.

Human data boundary

Data-use boundary

VHL does not treat public availability as a shortcut around ethics, data-use, privacy, or institutional duties.

Allowed

Public or authorized data

Data must be public or lawfully authorized for the specific research use, including compliance with repository terms, data-use agreements, and publication limits.

Review

Human-subject and biospecimen-derived data

Human-subject data, biospecimen-derived data, controlled-access datasets, and individual-level clinical information require applicable ethics review or exemption determinations and institutional authorization.

Prohibited

No unauthorized internal records

Patient records, military medical data, internal hospital or university data, company-confidential information, non-public institutional statistics, accounts, equipment, or facilities are not used without prior written authorization.

Intellectual property

Participation alone does not transfer rights.

Project-specific rights must be checked against actual contribution, law, institutional policies, funding terms, and written agreements.

Background rights

Background intellectual property remains with its original owner. Participation in VHL does not by itself transfer a member's, collaborator's, employer's, university's, hospital's, company's, or government organization's intellectual property to VHL.

Project outputs

Rights in project outputs are determined by actual contribution, applicable law, institutional policies, funding conditions, and project-specific written terms. Code, models, datasets, and documents are released under an open license only after ownership and third-party restrictions have been reviewed.

Reserved decisions

Patents, exclusive licenses, assignments, commercial priority rights, material data agreements, and institutional agreements require the collective approval specified in the Bylaws.
